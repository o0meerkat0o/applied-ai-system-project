Ambient music prioritizes texture, atmosphere, and mood over traditional melody and rhythm. It is designed to create a sonic environment rather than demand active listening. Energy levels are the lowest of any genre — ambient music is almost always quiet and slow, with tempos often below 70 BPM or entirely absent. Acousticness is high due to synthesizer pads, field recordings, and minimal percussion. Moods are typically chill, focused, or reflective. Ambient music is used for meditation, sleep, deep focus work, and anxiety reduction. Artists like Brian Eno, Moby, and Stars of the Lid define the genre. It is important to note that ambient and high-energy are fundamentally incompatible — the genre is defined by its low intensity.
