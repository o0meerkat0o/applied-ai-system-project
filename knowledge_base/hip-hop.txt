Hip-hop is a genre originating from African American and Latino communities in New York City in the 1970s. It is built around rhythmic vocal delivery (rapping) over sampled or produced beats. Energy levels are moderate to high and moods range widely from happy and celebratory to moody and introspective. Tempos typically sit between 85-100 BPM. Danceability is high. Acousticness is low due to electronic production, though lo-fi hip-hop sub-genres have higher acousticness from sampled instruments. Hip-hop listeners value authenticity, lyrical complexity, and cultural expression. Artists like Kendrick Lamar, J. Cole, and Drake represent the modern mainstream, while producers like J Dilla shaped its sonic identity.
