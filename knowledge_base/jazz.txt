Jazz is a genre characterized by improvisation, complex harmonies, and swing rhythms. It originated in African American communities in New Orleans in the early 20th century. Jazz listeners typically seek relaxed or sophisticated moods and low-to-moderate energy levels. Acousticness is high — jazz is dominated by acoustic instruments like piano, upright bass, trumpet, and saxophone. Tempos range from slow ballads (60 BPM) to fast bebop (200+ BPM), though most jazz standards sit around 80-120 BPM. Jazz is strongly associated with coffee shops, evening listening, and intellectual engagement. Artists like Miles Davis, John Coltrane, and Chet Baker define the genre's emotional range.
