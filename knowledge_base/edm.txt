Electronic Dance Music (EDM) is a broad genre built for high-energy environments like clubs, festivals, and intense workout sessions. It is characterized by synthesized sounds, powerful bass drops, and tempos typically between 128-145 BPM. EDM listeners tend to seek intense, euphoric moods and extremely high energy levels. Acousticness is near zero — the genre is entirely electronic. Danceability is among the highest of any genre. Sub-genres include house, techno, dubstep, and trance, each with slightly different energy profiles. Artists like Calvin Harris, Martin Garrix, and Skrillex define mainstream EDM. The genre is optimized for collective experience and physical movement.
