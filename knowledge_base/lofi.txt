Lo-fi hip hop (lofi) is a genre defined by its deliberately imperfect, warm sound aesthetic. It typically features slow tempos (60-90 BPM), mellow beats, ambient textures, and a chill or focused mood. Lofi is strongly associated with studying, reading, and late-night coding sessions. Listeners value the non-intrusive nature of the music — it is designed to be present without demanding attention. Acousticness is typically high due to sampled instruments and vinyl crackle textures. Energy levels are consistently low to moderate. The genre has a strong online community culture, often accompanied by animated lo-fi girl visuals on YouTube streams. Artists like Nujabes, J Dilla, and channels like ChilledCow define the aesthetic.
